## Interpretation

- `compact_roi` is currently conservative: only 4 of 24 compact cases returned success. This protects users from bad color transfer, but it is the largest capability gap.
- `too_wide_roi` is stable: all 24 cases were blocked as `roi_too_wide`.
- `too_narrow_roi` is mostly safe, but `jacket_hanger_003_too_narrow_roi` and `trouser_hanger_003_too_narrow_roi` returned local masks as success.
- White-background jacket, polo, and T-shirt no-ROI masks were visually strong. White-background trouser no-ROI cases were conservatively blocked as partial, while their compact ROIs succeeded.
- Two close-up no-ROI cases returned visually local/patchy masks as success, demonstrating that close-up success still needs stronger completeness validation.
- Jacket hanger samples 001 and 002 included a small support/metal region under the hem, so those no-ROI successes are not clean enough for automatic color transfer.

## Next Optimization Priorities

1. Improve compact ROI candidate selection without relaxing `roi_too_wide`, `over_coverage`, `partial`, or `low_confidence` blocking.
2. Add completeness checks for close-up and too-narrow success masks so local garment patches cannot pass as full usable masks.
3. Strengthen thin support/metal artifact rejection for hanger jackets.
4. Investigate why isolated trousers fail no-ROI while succeeding under compact ROI; keep the current safe block until the cause is understood.
5. Re-run this exact 96-case matrix after any algorithm change and compare both raw success and manual-valid success, not raw success alone.
